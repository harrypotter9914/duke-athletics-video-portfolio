Unreal Narrative Experience

Echo of Crash

An interactive experience built around road accidents, suspended moments, and post-impact investigation. Instead of treating collisions as pure spectacle, the design reframes them as scenes the player can read, reconstruct, and emotionally process through exploration.

Overview

Using the aftermath of impact as the real playable space.

Echo of Crash is structured around a strong framing device: the moment of collision opens into a suspended state where the player can move through the scene, inspect what happened, and understand why the crash occurred. That turns the experience from simple driving into layered narrative investigation.

The project is less interested in racing skill than in point of view. The player is asked to interpret behavior, consequences, and human error by walking through the evidence of each accident.

Core intent

Create a system where environmental storytelling and player curiosity carry the emotional weight of the experience.

My Role

I treated design, interaction, and implementation as one workflow.

Design Responsibilities

  • Defined the accident-investigation loop and how each scene reveals cause and consequence.
  • Built the player's movement between driving tension and spirit-like inspection mode.
  • Shaped environmental storytelling so players gather meaning by reading the scene.

Technical Responsibilities

  • Implemented scene logic and interactive behavior in Unreal Engine.
  • Connected pacing, camera language, and world interaction to narrative beats.
  • Built a playable structure where investigation supports emotion instead of interrupting it.

Design Focus

The important design problem was pacing, not only interaction.

State Shift

The transition from crash to investigation needed to feel legible and meaningful, not arbitrary. That state change is the project's core dramatic move.

Scene Readability

Players must be able to understand what to inspect, what matters narratively, and how details connect into a causal story.

Emotional Tone

The presentation avoids treating accidents as only action moments. Instead, the project leans on mood, stillness, and reconstruction to create empathy and reflection.
