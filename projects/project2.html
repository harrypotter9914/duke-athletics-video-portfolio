Overview

Building a world where progression, atmosphere, and mechanics reinforce each other.

Babel was designed as a world that players unfold piece by piece. Exploration combines movement skill, environmental reading, combat readiness, and narrative context. That structure made the game a natural fit for Metroidvania-style gating.

The setting and tone mattered just as much as the systems. The game aims to make the tower feel mythic, oppressive, and historically grounded enough to support the player's sense of danger and curiosity.

Core intent

Create a game where unlocking a new power changes both where the player can go and how the world feels to traverse.

My Role

I worked at the intersection of game design and technical implementation.

Design Responsibilities

  • Defined exploration flow, ability-gated progression, and combat-facing player growth.
  • Shaped how skills like Shockwave, Wall Jump, and Crystal Dash unlock space.
  • Built narrative framing so mechanical progression supports tone and story direction.

Technical Responsibilities

  • Worked within a custom engine and adapted design decisions to a nonstandard toolchain.
  • Contributed to systems implementation while coordinating with engine constraints.
  • Translated design needs into workable behaviors inside a more technical pipeline.

Design Focus

Each ability changes how the map opens up.

Ability-Gated Exploration

Each skill opens meaningful paths, changes encounter planning, and makes returning to older spaces feel purposeful.

Combat and Tension

The player's movement and offensive growth were paced together so combat escalation does not feel detached from traversal progression.

Atmosphere Through Structure

The architecture, enemy presence, and route reveal are used to support the feeling of climbing deeper into a dangerous place shaped by myth and collapse.

What mattered technically

  • Design decisions had to respect the constraints of a custom engine workflow.
  • Progression and moment-to-moment gameplay needed to stay coherent without editor convenience.
  • The project required collaboration between game-layer design and engine-level capability.
