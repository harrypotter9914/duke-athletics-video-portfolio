Fit to Pass

A mechanic-driven platformer where changing body size is both the core movement system and the thematic hook. The project was designed around readable challenge escalation, playful traversal, and turning a health-related idea into something players understand through action rather than exposition.

Overview

Turning a single mechanic into the whole game language.

The concept starts from a simple question: what if the player's body could become the main tool for reading and solving space? In Fit to Pass, size change is not a one-off gimmick. It becomes the rule that shapes corridor width, obstacle timing, jump rhythm, and how players scan a room before moving.

The result is a platformer that asks players to think physically. Instead of only reacting with jumps and dashes, they constantly decide what form is appropriate for the next passage, threat, or timing window.

Core intent

Make the mechanic legible in seconds, interesting over time, and tied closely enough to the level design that the game could not exist without it.

My Role

I handled both the design logic and the playable implementation.

Design

  • Defined the body-changing mechanic and how each state affects traversal.
  • Structured level flow around teaching, reinforcing, and combining mechanic rules.
  • Balanced puzzle readability with platforming pressure so the game stays active.

Implementation

  • Built the gameplay logic in Unity and connected interaction states to player controls.
  • Implemented level behavior, obstacle logic, and UI-facing feedback loops.
  • Translated design intent directly into playable systems without a separate handoff.

Design Focus

Readable challenge mattered more than raw difficulty.

Mechanic Clarity

The player should understand quickly why a certain body state works in a specific space. That meant clean silhouettes, deliberate obstacle spacing, and clear geometric relationships in the level layout.

Escalation

Levels move from isolated mechanic tutorials to situations where players must combine movement choices under pressure. The challenge comes from sequencing and timing, not from arbitrary confusion.

Theme Through Play

The health angle is carried through embodiment. Instead of long explanation, the game uses physical transformation as the player's direct connection to the idea.
